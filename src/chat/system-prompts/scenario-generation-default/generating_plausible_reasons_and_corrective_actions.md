*Module Purpose*
Ensure high-quality, realistic, and educationally valuable troubleshooting content.

*Module Content*
**For part (b) – Troubleshoot the Deviation:**
- Generate 2 or 3 distinct reasons.
- Each reason must be physically plausible and directly linked to the concepts being tested (mixing, side reactions, feed purity, sensor drift, heat loss, mass balance closure error, common-ion miscalculation, residence time insufficient, fouling, etc.).
- Reasons must explain **why the observed deviation occurs**, not generic student mistakes.

**Good examples from samples:**
- Cryolite yield low → incomplete reaction due to poor mixing, loss of product during filtration/drying, side reactions consuming Al2O3 or NaOH, actual Al2O3 mass lower than charged (weigh scale error).
- pH higher than target → less Pyridinium chloride added than calculated, wrong pKb used, dilution error, temperature effect on pKb not accounted for.
- Reaction slower than 2nd-order model → actual order different, catalyst poisoning, temperature lower than assumed, poor mixing reducing effective concentration.
- Energy use higher than theoretical → overpotential / faradaic losses higher than ideal, heat losses from stack, parasitic power draw from auxiliaries, lower electrolyser efficiency than design.

**For part (c) – Corrective Action:**
- Each action must be a concrete, practical engineering step an engineer could actually take (calibrate the mass balance / pH meter / flow meter, install static mixer or improve agitation, verify feed certificate of analysis and re-weigh, add temperature control or insulation, perform residence time distribution test, install online conductivity or pH sensor with alarm, etc.).
- Actions should be testable or verifiable.

**CRITICAL:** Never make the reasons or actions so obvious or educational that they give away the answer. Keep them at the level of a good exam question.
